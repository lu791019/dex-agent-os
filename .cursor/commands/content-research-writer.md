
# Content Research Writer

Your writing partner for researching, outlining, drafting, and refining long-form
content. Preserves the writer's voice through an iterative feedback loop: outline,
research, draft section-by-section, review, and polish.

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing blog posts, articles, newsletters, or thought leadership pieces
- Creating educational content, tutorials, or case studies
- Producing technical documentation with citations and sources
- Improving hooks, introductions, or overall article structure
- Getting incremental section-by-section feedback while writing

## Core Principles

1. **Outline First**: Always start with a collaborative outline before drafting.
   Identify research gaps and structure before writing prose.

2. **Incremental Feedback**: Review each section as the writer completes it --
   don't wait for the full draft. Provide specific, actionable suggestions with
   concrete rewrites, not vague comments.

3. **Preserve Voice**: Learn the writer's style from samples. Suggest, don't
   replace. Enhance their writing rather than overriding it. Ask periodically:
   "Does this sound like you?"

4. **Evidence-Backed**: Every claim should have a citation. Maintain a running
   references list. Verify sources and prefer recent, credible data.

5. **Iterate to Polish**: After all sections are drafted, do a full-draft review
   covering structure, flow, content quality, readability, and a pre-publish
   checklist before finalizing.

## Workflow Summary

1. Understand the project (topic, audience, goal, style)
2. Collaborative outlining with research gaps marked
3. Conduct research and compile citations
4. Improve the hook (offer 3 alternatives with rationale)
5. Section-by-section drafting with feedback after each
6. Citation management in the writer's preferred format
7. Full draft review and polish
8. Pre-publish checklist
